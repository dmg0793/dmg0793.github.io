## 1. Sigma-Algebras

### Definition 1.1 — sigma-field

Let $\Omega$ be a nonempty set. A family $\mathcal{F} \subseteq 2^\Omega$ is a **sigma-field** if:
1. $\Omega \in \mathcal{F}$
2. $A \in \mathcal{F} \implies A^c \in \mathcal{F}$
3. $A_n \in \mathcal{F} \implies \bigcup_{n=1}^\infty A_n \in \mathcal{F}$

### Example 1.2 — Trivial sigma-fields
- $\mathcal{F} = \{\varnothing, \Omega\}$: smallest sigma-field
- $\mathcal{F} = \mathcal{P}(\Omega)$: largest sigma-field

### Definition 1.3 — Generated sigma-field

$$\sigma(\mathcal{G}) := \bigcap\{\mathcal{F} : \mathcal{F} \text{ is a sigma-field}, \mathcal{G} \subseteq \mathcal{F}\}$$

### Example 1.4 — Borel sigma-field

$\mathcal{G} := \{(a,b] : a,b \in \mathbb{R}\} \Rightarrow \sigma(\mathcal{G}) = \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$

### Theorem 1.7 — Rational intervals generate Borel

$\sigma(\{(a,b): a,b \in \mathbb{Q}\}) = \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$

**Proof.** Any open set is a countable union of rational intervals. $\blacksquare$

### Theorem 1.9

$\sigma(\mathcal{I}') = \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$ (from $\mathcal{I} \subseteq \mathcal{I}' \subseteq \mathcal{T}$). $\blacksquare$

### Theorem 1.10

$\sigma(\mathcal{I}') = \sigma(\mathcal{I}'')$: Use $(a,b) = \bigcup_n [a+1/n, b-1/n]$ and $[a,b] = \bigcap_n (a-1/n, b+1/n)$. $\blacksquare$

## 2. Probability Spaces

### Definition 2.1 — Measure

$\mu: \mathcal{F} \to [0,\infty]$ is a **measure** if $\mu(\varnothing)=0$ and sigma-additive.

### Definition 2.3 — Probability space

$(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathbb{P})$ with $\mathbb{P}(\Omega)=1$.

### Theorem 2.4 — Properties of $\mathbb{P}$

**(a) Monotonicity:** $A \subseteq B \Rightarrow \mathbb{P}(A) \le \mathbb{P}(B)$.

**(b) Continuity from below:** $A_n \uparrow A \Rightarrow \lim_n \mathbb{P}(A_n) = \mathbb{P}(A)$.

**Proof.** (a) $\mathbb{P}(B) = \mathbb{P}(A) + \mathbb{P}(B\setminus A) \ge \mathbb{P}(A)$. (b) Disjointify: $B_n = A_n \setminus \bigcup_{j<n}A_j$, then sum telescopes. $\blacksquare$

**Remark 2.5.** $A_n \downarrow A \Rightarrow \lim_n \mathbb{P}(A_n) = \mathbb{P}(A)$.

## 3. Pi-systems and Lambda-systems

### Definition 3.1 — pi-system

$\mathcal{P}$ closed under finite intersections.

### Definition 3.2 — lambda-system

$\mathcal{L}$: contains $\Omega$, closed under complements and countable disjoint unions.

### Theorem 3.3

$\mathcal{F}$ is a pi-system and lambda-system $\Leftrightarrow$ $\mathcal{F}$ is a sigma-field. $\blacksquare$
